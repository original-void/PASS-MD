GENERAL BUILDING CONSTRUCTION

KNEC Notes - Module 1 | Units 1-5

📘 UNIT 1: INTRODUCTION TO CONSTRUCTION

Evolution to building environment

Environment = surroundings which can be natural, man-made or a combination of these.

Built Environment = created by man with or without the aid of the natural environment.

EVOLUTION OF BUILT ENVIRONMENT

  • 1) Pre-Historic dwellings: When man was a hunter & gatherer, dwellings were natural caves. With population increase, man carved more caves + constructed simple structures.
  • 2) Traditional dwellings: Development from simple shelters as population increased + industrialization adapted.
  • 3) Modern buildings: Use functional structural components i.e columns, beams, canopies and reinforced concrete slabs.

Stages involved in building progress

  • 1) Inception: Proper briefing of concerned parties
  • 2) Conception: Communication among design teams
  • 3) Select: Identification of construction site
  • 4) Construction: Actual work to come up with proposed structure

📍 UNIT 2: SITE INVESTIGATION

Site investigation - Activities to collect data systematically needed in design & construction process.

Data needed during site investigation

  • a) Site access i.e Roads, boundary lines, location
  • b) Existing services i.e sewer line, water drains, electricity, telephone
  • c) Adjoining structures i.e existing buildings, trees on adjacent site
  • d) Soil conditions - soil types, properties + ground water condition

Soil Investigation vs Site Investigation

Site Investigation - All-embracing term covering every aspect of site.

Soil Investigation - Specifically related to subsoil beneath site.

Purpose of Soil Investigation

  • 1. Determine suitability of site for proposed project
  • 2. Determine adequate + economic foundation design
  • 3. Determine difficulties during construction
  • 4. Determine occurrence/cause of changes in subsoil conditions

Soil Samples

Disturbed Soil Samples - From boreholes + trial pits. Structure disturbed but suitable for grading, moisture content + lab tests.

Undisturbed Soil Samples - Using boring tools that preserve natural structure. Suitable for rock + clay. Laid in wooden boxes for lab testing.

Methods of soil investigation

  • 1. Trial pits: Foundations up to 3,000mm deep
  • 2. Borings: Foundations up to 30,000mm deep
  • 3. Deep borings: Foundations over 30,000mm deep

Activities done during site clearance

  • I. Clearance of trees and bushes
  • II. Removal of vegetable soil/top soil
  • III. Removal of termite nets
  • IV. Demolition of unwanted structures

💥 DEMOLITION

I. Taking down

Careful removal of salvageable materials prior to complete destruction. Requires knowledge of load bearing members. May need propping, strutting + shoring.

II. Demolition

Complete removal of structural members. Survey of structure + neighbouring structures required. Photographs taken + stored safely.

Methods of demolition

  • Hand demolition: Using hand tools. NB: Objects should not freely drop if above ground.
  • Pusher arm: Machine with steel pusher arm. Needs firm base with 6 meters clear operating base.
  • Deliberate demolition: Removal of key member causing collapse. Expert advice required.
  • Ball technique: Swinging demolition ball by crane against structure.

📏 UNIT 3: LEVELLING

LEVELLING - Process of establishing heights, dimension relative to fixed point of data.

Tools/ equipment used

  • I. Building line/string, II. Hammer/mallet, III. Pegs, IV. Jembes, V. Spade, VI. Graders, bulldozers, rollers

Methods of levelling

I. Cut and fill: Amount of cut = amount of fill. Soil from upper part deposited on lower part.

II. Cut: Gives undisturbed ground. Disadvantage: cost of removing soil.

III. Fill: Not best. Risk of settlement, needs deeper foundation.

📍 UNIT 4: SETTING OUT

SETTING OUT - Establishment of marks + lines to determine position + level of construction elements.

First tasks

  • I. Baseline - All setting out relates to it. Coincides with building line given by local authority.
  • II. Site level/bench mark - Fixed point like drain cover or post for floor/drainage levels.

SETTING OUT METHODS

I. 3-4-5 Method

Uses Pythagoras theorem. Triangle 3m, 4m, 5m with right angle. Check by diagonals AC & DB must be equal. Advantages: Simple, no calculation, accuracy self-evident.

II. Builders Square Method

Place square at A, extend string = AD. Repeat at B. Check diagonals. Advantages: Very accurate for small rectangular buildings.

III. Surveying Instruments

Theodolite on tripod. Range limited to 33m. Most accurate for large buildings.

PROFILE BOARDS

Temporary timber framework to guide trench excavation. Posts sunk 450-600mm deep, 600-1000mm above ground. At least 4 nails outline wall thickness + trench width.

Circular Building

String and spike: Peg at centre, string tight, move around.

Trammel method: Not prone to inaccuracy of un-tightening string.

⛏️ EXCAVATION AND TIMBERING

Excavation

Excavation - Removal of earth to form cavity for foundation or basements.

Trench excavation

Battered faces: No temporary supports needed. Disadvantage: extra excavation + backfilling.

Straight faces: Minimum soil removed. Disadvantage: requires timbering.

Timbering

Timbering - Temporary supports on sides of trench to avoid collapsing. Amount depends on soil strength.

Factors to consider

  • I. Type of soil, II. Time open, III. Depth, IV. Load around, V. Vibration, VI. Weather - rain needs more support

Methods of protecting trench sides

  • a) Soils heaps, b) Side rail, c) Drum filled with soils + rail/ropes

🏗️ UNIT 5: FOUNDATIONS - COMPLETE

FOUNDATION

Foundation - Lower portion below ground level transmitting superstructure load to sub soil.

Total Loading

  • Live loads - Movable: furniture, human beings
  • Dead loads - Fixed: walls, roof, floors

Building code requirement

  • a) Safely sustain + transmit combined loads without settlement
  • b) Depth to avoid damage by swelling/shrinkage of subsoil
  • c) Resist chemical attack by sulphates/corrosive materials

Functions of foundation

  • Distribution of load - Spread load over large area
  • Minimizing unequal settlement - Equal settlement, no extra forces
  • Providing stability - Firm anchorage against lateral forces
  • Provide level surface - For plinth + superstructure
  • Safety against undermining - Floods + animal burrowing
  • Prevention of soil movement - Water flow + soil expansion

SETTLEMENT

Types:

  • Immediate: During construction. Non-cohesive soils.
  • Consolidated: Gradual, years after completion. Cohesive soils i.e clay.

Causes:

Deformation, seasonal swelling/shrinkage, mass movement, pile driving vibration, adjacent excavation, earthquakes, lowering/increasing water table, frost heave.

TYPES OF FOUNDATION

Shallow foundations ≤ 2000mm deep: Strip, Pad, Raft

Deep foundations: Pile, Pier

Strip foundations

Shallow/traditional: Good bearing capacity. NB: Working space 450-600mm. Depth ≥ wall width. Width = 3x wall width. 150mm hardcore base.

Reinforced/wide: Poor soil like soft sand, clay. Reinforced both directions.

Deep strip: 900mm deep. For shrinkable clayey soil.

Stepped: Sloping ground. Each step 150mm-225mm. Lap ≥ 300mm.

Raft foundation {Floating}

Large continuous footing, up to 300mm thick. For poor bearing capacity, differential settlement.

Types: Solid slab ≤300mm, Beam & slab >300mm, Cellular >900mm beams.

Advantages: Car park, storage, services, supermarkets.
